Government initiatives

National schemes that support children and adults with disabilities and neurodevelopmental conditions — what each one covers, and where to apply. Every link goes to the official government page.

Show:

Samagra Shiksha

Preschool to secondary

An integrated education scheme supporting children with diverse learning needs from preschool to secondary level. It promotes inclusive education by placing children in mainstream schools with tailored support.

Visit official site (opens in a new tab)

DISHA Scheme

Up to age 10

Provides early intervention and school readiness for children up to 10 years old with developmental or intellectual challenges covered under the National Trust Act. Includes therapies, training, and family support.

Visit official site (opens in a new tab)

Home-Based Education (RTE Act)

School age

Enables children with significant and multiple support needs to receive education at home, ensuring their right to learn in a safe and accessible environment.

Learn more (opens in a new tab)

VIKAAS Scheme

Age 10 and above

A structured daycare program for individuals over 10 years old with cognitive or developmental conditions. Focuses on building interpersonal and vocational skills in a supportive setting.

Visit official site (opens in a new tab)

Deendayal Rehabilitation Scheme (DDRS)

For organizations

Offers financial support to NGOs to run inclusive schools and community programs for individuals with varied support needs, fostering empowerment and access.

Visit official site (opens in a new tab)

Niramaya Health Insurance

All ages

Provides affordable health insurance for individuals with developmental or intellectual conditions, covering therapies, diagnostics, and ongoing care without pre-screening.

Visit official site (opens in a new tab)

Gyan Prabha

Students & trainees

A scholarship scheme offering financial aid for education and vocational training to individuals with neurodevelopmental conditions, encouraging skill-building and independence.
